Using VR Tools to Playtest Museum Installations

Daryl Choa --Carnegie Mellon’s Entertainment Technology Center

Session Description

Given 15 weeks and a team of five students, how do you build a robust prototype for a mixed reality experience that you will neither fabricate or install? One Small Act was a collaboration between Carnegie Mellon’s Entertainment Technology Center and the Children’s Museum of Pittsburgh to develop an interactive experience to practice kindness, for the Museum’s new exhibit opening in October 2019. The team will share their insights from using VR tools to rapidly prototype and playtest interactions, to how it allowed them to develop a meaningful experience for children and caregivers of all ages.

Bio

Daryl Choa is a second-year masters student at Carnegie Mellon’s Entertainment Technology Center. There she is studying how to design transformational interactive experiences that are engaging, accessible, and culturally relevant.
