M-PAVE: Maryland Project on Avatars and Virtual Environments

Dr. Helene Kalson Cohen --University of Maryland - College Park

Session Description

The College of Education trains teachers, counselors, and administrators by using avatars who look, behave, and respond like the students and parents they will encounter in real-life situations. These VR simulations, developed and supported by Mursion, Inc., are a blend of artificial intelligence and live human interaction. They are not just appropriate for training educators, but for any occupation where professionals need to practice and master essential skills to be effective in high-stakes, highly interactive careers like health care, customer service, business and defense. You will leave the presentation able to clearly envision the VR interactions and intrigued by the possibilities for use in your particular setting.

Bio

Helene Kalson Cohen is the Executive Director of the Office of Innovative Technology and Partnerships in the College of Education at the University of Maryland.
