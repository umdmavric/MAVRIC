Blame the Design Team

Jeff Fiske --Muzzy Lane Software

Session Description

"The session is geared towards experienced developers or any teams in software development. Attendees will gain tools, process & procedures to examine their own projects to improve the outcome through focused design. Overview: Fast paced media-rich overview of key elements on why simulations work. If simulations work so well, why are so few products successful? There are two facets to design that threaten to undermine every development team; designing for the wrong customer and losing focus. Making a product that achieves desired outcomes at the development studio is not enough. Meeting the needs of your customers where and how they are actually going to use it, has to be designed in from the start. These are not the same thing. A great product that meets design goals from the developer standpoint will fail if the end consumer doesn’t have the tech, time, or training or myriad other logistical reasons to use as intended. As developers, we don’t spend enough time really designing for who the customer actually is and the logistics of how they actually will use the product. We often ask our partners/publishers questions about what we should do, that they don’t really understand and we are surprised six months later when they finally understand the question and want to change their answer. Feature creep is a natural byproduct of creative developers who have appropriate leeway to develop their tools with the best possible outcome in mind but we do little to rein it in. Solution: Discuss and explain how identifying the true customer and a single design feature as foundation must guide the entire development process with LASER focus. A clear picture answers the questions for everyone involved at every step along the way. Review specific ways to do this, using tools like workflowy (https://workflowy.com/s/EVia.VFavOWfKTz), Agile, Scrum, Kanban or whatever planning tools the teams use. These tools only help when used properly to set the foundation and build with focus to the end goal. Additional steps will be revealed on how to map out top level goals while remaining on track with iterative development. At the end, I will give an example by showing how creating a rapid prototype take help everyone involved in the project understand where it is at and communicate more effectively the direction it will need to go. "
